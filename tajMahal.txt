Emperor Shahjahan built Taj Mahal as a symbol of love.
It is situated in Agra.
It was named after his wife Mumtaj Mahal.
Mumtaj was his 13 th wife.
The architecture was commissioned in 1632.
The architecture is the centrepiece of a 17 hectare complex.
It was completed at a cost of around 32 million rupees at that time.
The construction project employed some 20000 artisans.
Ustad Ahmad Lahouri was the proud architect of the architecture.
The Taj Mahal was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983.
The Taj Mahal incorporates and expands on design traditions of Persian Mughal architecture.
The Ninety Nine Names of Allah are calligraphic inscriptions on the sides of the actual tomb of Mumtaz Mahal.
